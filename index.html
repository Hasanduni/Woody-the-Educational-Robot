Research Overview

Adaptive Explainable HRC for Intelligent Sorting

This website presents our research as both a rigorous academic project and a polished innovation showcase. Through this platform, we demonstrate our proposed solution for inclusive learning, integrating robotic sorting, gaze-based object selection, vision-based material recognition, and explainable AI-driven recommendations into a unified human-robot collaboration framework.

Abstract

The proposed system introduces a human-in-the-loop intelligent learning environment centered on Woody the Educational Robot. It integrates a DINOv2-based material recognition module for material classification, YOLO-powered object detection and scene understanding for identifying and localizing target objects, a Gaze and head pose intention estimation module using RGB cameras for intuitive human–robot interaction, and an explainable AI predictive sorting component that provides transparent decision support for robotic actions. Together, these four components form a multimodal collaborative framework that enables perception, intention recognition, explainability, and adaptive robotic assistance. The system supports not only intelligent object sorting and educational interaction, but also promotes trust, user engagement, cognitive learning, and accessible human–robot collaboration through transparent and assistive decision-making.

Domain

Research foundation, methodology, and technical stack

The domain section highlights the key research areas, identified problem, system methodology, and the technologies that power the intelligent sorting platform.

📚

Literature Survey

Prior work in robotic sorting, HRC, educational robotics, gaze-based intention recognition, and explainable AI demonstrates strong progress, but practical systems still struggle with adaptability, affordability, and human-centered transparency.

🕳️

Research Gap

Existing systems often rely on expensive hardware, fragmented modules, or industrial-only settings. Few solutions combine material recognition, gaze interaction, recommendation logic, and explainability in one inclusive educational platform.

⚠️

Problem Statement

Traditional educational and robotic interaction systems lack affordable, adaptive, and transparent mechanisms that support differently abled learners through natural multimodal collaboration.

🎯

Objectives

Build an intelligent sorting environment that supports material classification, gaze-based intention detection, object-based learning tasks, and explainable recommendation-driven robotic behavior.

🧪

Methodology

The workflow integrates data capture, preprocessing, DINOv2 material recognition, YOLO detection, ResNet‑18 gaze classification, region mapping, Random Forest sorting recommendations, and SHAP explanations.

💻

Technologies

Python, PyTorch, OpenCV, NumPy, YOLOv8, DINOv2, ResNet‑18, Random Forest, SHAP, Logistic Regression, Arduino-based robot control, RGB camera streams, and web-based monitoring interfaces.
